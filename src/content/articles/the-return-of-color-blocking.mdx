## A Reckoning with Color

For the better part of three years, fashion has been in a state of chromatic hibernation. The palette of the early 2020s — oatmeal, mushroom, stone, sand — served a cultural moment that demanded seriousness. But seasons change, and this season, the runways have made one thing unmistakably clear: color is back.

Not the cautious, desaturated color of recent memory, but the real thing. Cobalt. Vermilion. Emerald. Canary yellow in concentrations that would have seemed aggressive eighteen months ago.

### Where It Came From

The shift began, as these shifts often do, on the margins. Small houses in Lagos and Accra never abandoned color — they simply were not invited to the conversation when muted minimalism dominated the editorial pages. Designers like Thebe Magugu, Kenneth Ize, and Mowalola have been working in vivid palettes throughout fashion's beige period. Now the establishment is catching up.

> "Color is not frivolous. Color is political. When you wear bright color, you are making a claim on space and attention."
>
> — Thebe Magugu

### The Rules of Engagement

The new color blocking is not the haphazard rainbow of the 2010s. It is deliberate, precise, and often limited to two or three colors per look. The trick is in the relationships between the colors — the unexpected harmonies that arise when you put unlikely neighbors together in a single garment or outfit.

The most compelling combinations from the Spring collections:

- **Cobalt and Chartreuse** — cold blue against electric yellow-green. The contrast is jarring at first, then addictive.
- **Terracotta and Lavender** — earth meeting atmosphere. A combination that feels both ancient and futuristic.
- **Acid Green and Magenta** — the club-kid palette, now refined for daylight. Best executed in matte fabrics that absorb rather than reflect.
- **Oxblood and Saffron** — deep red meeting deep yellow. Rich, almost edible, like the inside of a well-stocked pantry.

### How to Wear It

For those who have spent the last few years building a wardrobe of neutrals, the transition to color need not be abrupt. The most elegant approach is the single statement piece — a cobalt blazer over an otherwise neutral outfit, or emerald trousers with a white shirt and sandals.

The confidence comes from the editing. Color blocking works when the silhouette remains simple and the fabric choice remains considered. Let the colors do the talking.

### What It Means

Fashion's turn toward color is more than an aesthetic choice. It reflects a cultural shift — a collective decision that the austerity of the recent past no longer serves us. Color is optimism made visible, a refusal to disappear into the background.

This season, the most radical thing you can wear is the color you've been avoiding.
